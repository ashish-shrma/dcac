Four Year Undergraduate Programme 2013-14




Principal's take on Four Year Undergraduate Program (FYUP)


Four Year Undergraduate Program (FYUP) has been introduced with a clear vision. It gives emphasis on the natural development and growth of the students instead of artificial grooming. It aims at creating self awareness among students so that they can take correct decisions at correct time. It also aims at designing higher education systems which will strengthen social norms and traditions on one hand and good international relations on the other.


The FYUP is based on belief in the principle of equal right for all. Hence, it promotes and facilitates subjective strength and individual specific potentials and knowledge foundation with requisite skills. It has given an opportunity to students with multiple degree options which is based on inter-disciplinary perspective. Successful pursuit of the four year program leads to the award of the bachelor with honors degree. Students will have the option to pursue a three year program leading to an award of bachelor degree and the option to pursue a two year program leading to the award of associate bachelor degree. Students who obtain a bachelor degree or associate bachelor degree shall have the option to rejoin the university within a time frame to continue their studies leading to the award of the bachelor degree and bachelor of the honors degree. It has given opportunity to colleges also to float their own courses. The student experiences a fine blend of virtual and actual class room where a teacher is not simply a regulator but a mentor and facilitator of learning process. Teaching is project based where student gets ample chance to prove themselves.


The focus is on holistic development of student through consistent input on mind body and heart. The significance of FYUP can be seen by the development of the projects by the students of my college on their own. I would like to mention projects like: Alternate Theory for Big Bang, Measurement of Zero Kelvin, Replacement of Chemical and Complete Business Model, Solar Water Distillation and Generation of Electricity, Hidden Monuments of Delhi, Slums of Delhi, Fruteos and Old Forts and Monuments in Delhi.





Good Practices of FYUP




Science and Life


Science and life foundation course has been committed to encourage students to observe, think and comprehend information in a logically consistent matter. This course has been redefining both for students and faculty, the outlook towards science. The projects have been innovative, and of original nature..


Some of innovative projects that we would like to mention:.


1) Alternate theory of big bang was an attempt to explain conservation of energy in initial conditions of universe and possible explanation of the dark energy, viewed from multidimensional multi-universe.


2) Alternate chemical and its complete business idea, attempted to alter harmful chemicals that we use in our daily life and it presented complete cost of projects and marketing strategies.


3) Cheapest Mid-day meal of India, attempted to look into reducing cost of the mid-day meal that government is spending with propagating any malnutrition.


4) There were projects in which students had to conceptualize machines that were totally innovative, in which students talked solar water distillation and electricity generation by trapping sunlight. In another, attempt they used motion of body to produce energy.


5) Students attempted to study, use of superconductors to study measurement of zero kelvin temperature.


We hope to produce more quality work and remain sincere in our attempt to develop scientific temper rather than science information.





Building Mathematical Ability


Patterns constantly emerge in our lives which can and should be studied. Sometimes we even see patterns where none exist; a phenomenon called apophenia. Pattern recognition and assessment form a link between the areas suggested for study in the Foundation Course Building Mathematical Ability..


In the study of prime numbers, matrices and encryption we try to see if any patterns emerge. When we discuss data collection and statistics we try to avoid any biases by avoiding certain templates. Finally, in probability theory we distinguish between patterns that actually exist from the ones that only appear to but are not reliable.


It is through open and free discussions in class that we step beyond the boundaries of the course where students and teachers alike break the traditional shackles of thought while exploring uncharted territories. Then students begin to see the harmful and the useful constraints on their own patterns of thought that have bound them thus far and break the ones they choose. They learn to start thinking for themselves and that is the goal of the Foundation Courses and this is how we achieve that at the Mathematics Department of the Delhi College of Arts and Commerce.
